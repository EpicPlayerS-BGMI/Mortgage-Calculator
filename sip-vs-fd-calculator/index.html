SIP vs FD ‚Äì Investment Comparison Explained

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds, benefiting from market growth and compounding. Fixed Deposits (FD), on the other hand, offer guaranteed returns with low risk but limited growth potential.

This SIP vs FD calculator compares both investment options using your input values to estimate maturity amounts. It helps investors understand how market-linked returns differ from fixed interest investments over time.

SIP vs FD ‚Äì Frequently Asked Questions

Is SIP better than FD?
SIP generally offers higher returns over the long term, while FD provides stable and guaranteed returns.
Is SIP risky?
SIP investments are market-linked and may fluctuate in the short term but tend to perform well over longer periods.
Can I invest in both SIP and FD?
Yes, combining SIP and FD can help balance risk and stability in your portfolio.
